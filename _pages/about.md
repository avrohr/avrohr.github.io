I’m a postdoctoral researcher at the [University of Technology Nuremberg](https://www.utn.de/) in the Foundations of Machine Learning group. I work on uncertainty-aware machine learning and optimization. I am mostly interested in Bayesian optimization and safe, robust reinforcement learning.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the [Technical University of Munich](https://www.tum.de/) with the [Learning Systems and Robotics Lab](https://www.learnsyslab.org) led by [Angela Schoellig](https://www.dynsyslab.org/prof-angela-schoellig/) and affiliated with the [Robotics Institute Germany](https://robotics-institute-germany.de/).

I did my PhD at the [Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems](https://is.mpg.de/) and [RWTH Aachen University](https://www.rwth-aachen.de/), advised by [Sebastian Trimpe](https://www.dsme.rwth-aachen.de/cms/DSME/Das-Institut/Team-CMS-Artikel-/~jlolt/Prof-Sebastian-Trimpe). During my PhD, I was an associated scholar of the International Max Planck Research School for Intelligent Systems ([IMPRS-IS](https://imprs.is.mpg.de/)).

Before that, I studied **Computer Science** at the University of Lübeck and earned a Bachelor’s degree in **Electrical Engineering** from BHT Berlin. In between, I worked full-time as a **Software Engineer** in Hamburg.

You can find my papers on the [Publications page](https://avrohr.com/publications/) and more about my teaching [here](https://avrohr.com/teaching/).
